What is fidelity in UI design?

Fidelity refers to the level of detail on a wireframe.

There are low, medium, and high fidelity wireframes.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Uses of low fidelity wireframes

Serve as a checkpoint for the product team and stakeholders at the beginning of the design process.

Help teams visualize and test early concepts, requirements and design assumptions at the beginning of a web design project.

Wireframes

What are high fidelity wireframes?

Also referred to as prototypes

Closely resembles the final design of a project

It can include typography, colors, images, icons, and CTA buttons.

High Fidelity Wireframes

How to high fidelity wireframes

Using software like Adobe XD, Sketch, etc.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Uses of high fidelity wireframes

Often built in the advanced stages of the design process to communicate design decisions.

Help validate complex interactions during user testing.

In summary

Low fidelity

  • Capturing ideas
  • Focuses on function not aesthetics
  • Works out flow before costly content
VS

High fidelity

  • Offers look and feel of final product
  • More accurate for user testing
  • Delivers design requirements for developers
Limitations

Low fidelity

  • Lacks full interactivity
  • Inadequate for unmoderated testing
  • Less inspiring than hi-fi
VS

High fidelity

  • Needs a longer timeline
  • Requires graphics and content
  • Iteration takes time and effort
Other than designers,

who else uses wireframe?

Stakeholder, developers, content writers, etc.
